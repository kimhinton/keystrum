# Keywords — ASO

## Apple App Store (100-char limit, comma-separated)

### Primary (100 chars exactly)
```
guitar,strum,chord,keyboard,music,instrument,karplus,web audio,synth,practice,qwerty,folk,record
```
Count (with separators): 99

### Keyword selection rationale
- `guitar` — highest-volume music term; anchors the category
- `strum` — unique to this product in the App Store; low-competition niche
- `chord` — music learners
- `keyboard` — differentiator vs piano apps
- `music instrument` — broad category signal
- `karplus` — technical niche, brings devs/synth enthusiasts
- `web audio` — dev niche
- `synth` — electronic music audience
- `practice` — music learning intent
- `qwerty` — unique mechanic keyword
- `folk` — songs in practice mode are folk
- `record` — recording-capability hint

### Keywords to AVOID
- `instrument` alone (already in Category; wastes chars)
- `app` / `music app` (Apple strips these automatically)
- Competitor names (Apple rejects brand keywords)

## Google Play tags

Google Play doesn't have an explicit keyword field (the description is indexed).
To maximize relevance, ensure the following terms appear in the full description:
- guitar, strum, chord, keyboard, instrument, karplus-strong, synth, practice, folk, record, share, offline, open source, MIT, Web Audio

The description in `descriptions.md` already naturally includes all of the above.

## Monthly keyword review

Re-score keyword performance after 30 days via App Store Connect's "Search Ads Keyword Planner" (even without buying ads). Swap out 2-3 lowest-performing keywords.

DO NOT change keywords more than once per month — burn rate signals category instability to Apple's algorithm.
